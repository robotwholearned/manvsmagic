Parents Don't Really Pay That Much Attention To The Details - Jul 14, 2014

A young man dressed in tradition magician garb is driving with his mother to camp. 'This is going to be the best summer ever! I’ve been practicing all my tricks plus I bet I’ll learn a ton from the other magicians!' says the boy excitedly. 'Don’t have so much fun at wizard camp that you forget about your dad and I. We’re going to miss you. ' says the mother. 'Magic camp' corrects the boy, 'Hmm? Sorry?' the mother says puzzled. 'You said wizard camp, but I’m going to magic camp.' clarifies the boy. 'Sure, whatever.' says the mom nonchalantly. 'Mom, this is super important. Did you sign me up for wizard camp or magic camp?' says the boy with concern in his voice. Next we see the boy dropped off at camp in front of what appears to be another young boy dressed up like a student of Hogwarts academy. 'Tarantallegra!' says the young wizard, 'God damn it mom' says the boy disappointedly.
